Brain Computer Interface Advance Allows Fast and Accurate Typing By People With Paralysis

The use of Brain Computer Interface has been a great influence on people with paralysis. Advances in technology allowed three people who have movement impairments to control the cursor of a computer by imagining that their hands were moving. This form of technology has helped people with paralysis to type through direct brain control. After being trained, the participants in this survey were able to outperform the results of any brain computer interface being done previously. One participant was able to type 39 characters per minute. Amazing right? How does it all work? They each had baby-aspirin-sized electrode arrays placed in their brains to record signals from the engine cortex, an area controlling muscle development. These signs were transmitted to a PC by means of a link and made an interpretation by calculations into point-and-snap charges managing a cursor to characters on an onscreen console. Based on the success of this study, researchers are looking into using brain computer interface for other impairments.
